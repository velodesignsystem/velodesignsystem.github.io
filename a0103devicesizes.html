iphone 8, iphone x and Samsung S8
Our design system and component library was created to be platform agnostic. This approach allows use to leverage a single designâ€™s components and behaviors for both the iOS platform and the Android platform.

The dashboard screens above show the dashboard page for iphone 8, iphone X and Samsung S8. Dashboard is the only screen in the app that is vertically responsive, all other screens are anchored to the top of the screen.

The Samsung S8 screen above has its screen size proportionally increased to match the with of iphone 8 and iphone X. This was done to reduce design efforts to redesign and resize components. Android developers can scale down this size, multiplying by 0.96 to return to actual Samsung S8 size (360x740).
Top anchored screens
All screens in the app (with the exception of dashboard, are top anchored, meaning that content extends down a page in the same way from the top of the page. If we see the images above, the experiences between the three devices are almost identical with the exception of plaform specific components.
iphone 8 to Samsung S8 conversion
The conversion from iphone 8 to Samsung S8 is identical, with the exception of a slightly different header component with OS native status bar, and the andoird navigation bar at the bottom of the page.

All vertical spacing remains the same, since both iphone 8 and Sansung S8 header components are the same height.
iphone 8 to iphone X
The conversion from iphone 8 to iphone X is very similar, with a 12px adjustment in vertical height on iphone X due to a taller header component to accomodate the notch. There is also a home indicator bar at the bottom of the screen.
